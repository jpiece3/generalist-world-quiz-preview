Sales Conversation Scripts

Conversation frameworks for following up with quiz leads at each temperature -- Hot, Warm, and Cold. Written in Milly's warm, genuine voice. Never pushy.

Score 80-100

Hot Lead Script

These leads have high clarity, confidence, and momentum. They've likely invested in career development before. They need community and acceleration -- not education.

Opening (DM, Email, or Call)

You:
Hi {{name}} -- I saw you took the Career Blocker Quiz and scored in the top range. Your results show you've already done a lot of the hard work. What's the biggest thing on your plate career-wise right now?
Lead:
(They'll typically talk about wanting to connect with peers, build a portfolio career, or accelerate a transition they've already started.)
Hot leads don't need to be sold on generalism. They already get it. Focus on WHAT'S NEXT, not WHY.

Discovery

You:
That makes total sense. One thing I hear a lot from generalists at your stage is that the biggest gap isn't skills or strategy -- it's the peer group. Are you currently connected with other generalists who are operating at your level?
Lead:
(Most will say no -- this is the core pain point for hot leads.)
You:
Yeah, that's really common. High-performing generalists often plateau without peer challenge. You need people who push you, not people you're explaining yourself to.

Recommendation

You:
Based on your quiz results and what you've shared, I think the Generalist World Community would be the right fit. It's 570+ people -- fractional executives, portfolio careerists, founders -- who are building the kind of career you're describing. There are live workshops, accountability groups, and curated job opportunities that match generalist strengths. Would you like me to tell you more about how it works?
Never lead with the price. Lead with the peer group. Hot leads buy access to people, not information.

Close

You:
The next community cohort opens soon. I can get you on the waitlist so you're first to know when doors open. There's a 30-day money-back guarantee, so there's no risk in trying it. Want me to add you?
Score 50-79

Warm Lead Script

These leads have identified as generalists but have specific gaps. They need frameworks and tools -- not just community. Focus on their specific blocker.

Opening

You:
Hi {{name}} -- thanks for taking the Career Blocker Quiz. Your results showed that your biggest area to work on is {{profile_name}}. Does that feel accurate to you?
Lead:
(They'll usually confirm or elaborate on their situation.)
Always validate their quiz result first. Let THEM confirm it's accurate. This builds trust in the diagnostic and makes your recommendation feel personalised.

Discovery

You:
Tell me more about that. When did you first notice this was holding you back?
Lead:
(Let them talk. The more they describe their situation, the more invested they become in finding a solution.)
You:
That makes sense. I hear this a lot from generalists -- you have all the skills, but the system for translating them is missing. It's not a YOU problem. It's a framework problem.

Recommendation (Blocker-Specific)

If Positioning Gap:

You:
For positioning specifically, we have the Positioning Guidebook -- it's a practical guide you can work through in about 3 hours. It helps you craft your generalist narrative, build your portfolio, and answer "what do you do?" with confidence. Would you like me to send you the link?

If Strategy or Focus Gap:

You:
For the strategy piece, Unboxed is our career design program. It gives you a structured process -- map your skills, run career experiments, build your career flywheel. The modules are bite-sized, 20 minutes or less. Would you like to hear more about how it works?

If Belonging Gap:

You:
The isolation piece is real. If that's your main blocker, the community might actually be a better fit than a course. But I'd also recommend starting with the newsletter and Archetype Quiz -- they'll give you a sense of what the community is like, and you can join when you're ready.

No-Pressure Close

You:
No rush on any of this. The newsletter goes out every week with free frameworks -- that's honestly a great place to start. When you're ready for the next step, everything I mentioned will still be here. Squiggly careers don't follow timelines.
Score 0-49

Cold Lead Script

These leads are in the early stages -- they may not even identify as generalists yet. Focus on validation and education. Never sell to cold leads.

Opening

You:
Hi {{name}} -- I noticed you took the Career Blocker Quiz. First, I just want to say: the fact that you took it means you're already further along than you think. What made you curious enough to take it?
Lead:
(They'll often say something like "I saw it on TikTok" or "A friend shared it." They may not use the word "generalist" yet.)
With cold leads, your only job is to validate their experience and introduce the generalist identity. Do NOT mention products, prices, or community. Just help them name what they're feeling.

Validation & Education

You:
Can I ask -- have you ever felt like your career path looks "messy" compared to the people around you? Like everyone else has one clear thing and you have... many things?
Lead:
(This is almost always a strong "yes" -- it's their core pain point.)
You:
That's actually a really common experience. There's a name for it: you're a generalist. Not scattered, not unfocused -- multi-dimensional. There are millions of people like this, and 47,000+ of them read our weekly newsletter because someone finally put words to what they've always felt.

Gentle Next Step

You:
If you're curious about exploring this more, I'd suggest two things. First, the weekly newsletter -- it's free and it's packed with career frameworks for people with your wiring. And second, the Archetype Quiz -- it tells you what KIND of generalist you are. It pairs really well with the Career Blocker results you already have.
The best outcome for a cold lead conversation is that they subscribe to the newsletter and take the Archetype Quiz. That's a win. Do not push further.
